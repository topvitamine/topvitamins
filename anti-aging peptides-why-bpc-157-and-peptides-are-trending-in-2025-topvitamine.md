What are peptides and how do they work?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids that act as signaling molecules, modulators of repair processes, and building blocks for proteins. Their relatively small size compared with full proteins allows some peptides to interact with receptors, modulate enzymatic pathways, or support structural proteins such as collagen. Supplement formulations commonly include peptides for skin support, joint health, or immune modulation.

Why are peptides more visible in 2025?

Several factors explain the renewed interest: improved research methodologies that clarify mechanisms of action, refined manufacturing techniques that increase purity, and consumer demand for targeted, biologically based interventions. Regulatory attention and better quality control have also reduced variability among products, increasing clinician and consumer confidence.
