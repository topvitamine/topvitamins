## What is liposomal vitamin C?

Liposomal vitamin C encapsulates ascorbic acid within microscopic lipid vesicles called liposomes, composed primarily of phospholipids similar to cell membranes. This encapsulation can reduce exposure of the vitamin to stomach acid and enzymes, and it may facilitate transport across the intestinal barrier via fusion with enterocyte membranes. Mechanistically, liposomal delivery aims to increase plasma and tissue concentrations of vitamin C compared with equivalent doses of unencapsulated ascorbic acid.

## Absorption and comparative effectiveness

Conventional oral vitamin C absorption is saturable: intestinal transporters limit uptake at higher doses and excess is typically excreted in urine. Clinical and pharmacokinetic studies indicate that liposomal formulations can achieve higher blood plasma levels than standard oral tablets or powders at comparable or lower doses. This does not necessarily mean identical clinical outcomes in every context, but enhanced plasma availability is a plausible mechanism for improved tissue delivery.
