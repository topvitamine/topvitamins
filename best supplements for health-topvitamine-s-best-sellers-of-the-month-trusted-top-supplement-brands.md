<p>High-demand vitamins typically address well-established nutritional gaps. Vitamin D is commonly recommended in clinical guidelines when sun exposure or dietary intake is insufficient, given its role in bone health and immune function. Vitamin C and B-complex formulations are also frequent choices: vitamin C supports antioxidant capacity and connective tissue maintenance, while B vitamins contribute to energy metabolism and neurological function. Evidence-based reviews underline that supplementation can be beneficial when dietary intake does not meet requirements.</p>

<p>Minerals such as magnesium and zinc appear often among top sellers because they participate in numerous physiological processes. Magnesium supports muscle and nerve function and contributes to sleep regulation; zinc is important for immune competence and enzymatic reactions. Bioavailability—how well a nutrient is absorbed and utilized—is a key factor differentiating formulations, so many consumers look for products with forms that research suggests are better absorbed.</p>

<p>Probiotics are another regularly purchased category, reflecting growing interest in gut health and the gut–brain axis. Clinical studies indicate strain-specific effects for digestive comfort and certain gastrointestinal conditions; therefore, strain identification, colony-forming unit (CFU) counts, and storage requirements are relevant product attributes to assess. Consumers benefit from transparent labeling and peer-reviewed evidence supporting a strain’s intended use.</p>
