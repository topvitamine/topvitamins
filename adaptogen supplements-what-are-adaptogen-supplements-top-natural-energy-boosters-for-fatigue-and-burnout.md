# What Are Adaptogens? Top Supplements to Fight Fatigue and Burnout

Fatigue and burnout are increasingly common problems related to chronic stress, disrupted sleep and high workloads. Adaptogen supplements are a class of botanicals traditionally used to help the body maintain balance during physical and emotional stress. Research suggests they may modulate stress-response pathways and support more stable energy and mood over time.

## Understanding Adaptogens

Adaptogens are natural herbs and roots historically used in Ayurvedic and traditional Chinese medicine. They are thought to act on the hypothalamic–pituitary–adrenal (HPA) axis and related neuroendocrine systems, helping regulate stress hormones such as cortisol. This regulatory effect may contribute to improved resilience, reduced perception of stress and more consistent energy levels compared with stimulants that produce short-term spikes.

## Mechanisms and evidence

Controlled studies vary by herb, but some adaptogens have moderate evidence for stress reduction and improved fatigue management. For example, ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) has been associated with reductions in perceived stress and cortisol in randomized trials, while Rhodiola rosea trials indicate potential benefits for fatigue and mental performance under stress. Mechanistically, adaptogens may influence neurotransmitter systems, antioxidant defenses and endocrine responses to promote homeostasis.

## Common adaptogens and their properties

- Ashwagandha: Often studied for anxiolytic effects and improved sleep quality, which can indirectly restore daytime energy.
- Rhodiola rosea: Associated with increased mental clarity and endurance; commonly used to combat fatigue during prolonged mental or physical demands.
- Holy Basil (Tulsi): Traditionally used for stress relief and mild anti-inflammatory effects, supporting overall resilience.

## Complementary natural supplements for fatigue

Adaptogens can be part of a broader nutritional strategy. Micronutrients involved in energy metabolism and nervous system function include B-complex vitamins and vitamin D; magnesium supports muscle and nerve health; and probiotics may influence energy and mood via the gut–brain axis. Combining evidence-based micronutrient support with adaptogens can address multiple contributors to fatigue.

## Choosing and using adaptogen supplements safely

Quality and standardization matter: look for products from reputable sources with transparent labeling and third-party testing. Follow recommended dosages on labels and consider starting at a lower dose to assess tolerance. Mild gastrointestinal symptoms can occur with some herbs, and interactions are possible if you take prescription medications or have certain health conditions; consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice.
