## Key red flags to watch for

- Implausible claims: Phrases such as “miracle cure,” “clinically proven” without citation, or “guaranteed rapid results” are common marketing devices. Scientific claims should reference peer-reviewed studies or regulatory registers.
- Suspicious reviews: Clusters of overly positive, repetitive testimonials posted in short timeframes often indicate review manipulation. Tools and services can help detect fake review patterns.
- Packaging inconsistencies: Misspellings, blurred printing, missing safety seals, or mismatched lot numbers may indicate counterfeit goods.
- Proprietary blends and vague labels: When ingredient amounts are withheld under “proprietary” listings, it is impossible to verify effective dosing or safety.

## Fake reviews and endorsements

Online testimonials shape purchase decisions, but they are also exploited. Some sellers use review farms, incentivized influencers, or scripted endorsements that offer no real evidence of efficacy. Look for specific details in reviews (how long someone used the product, measurable outcomes, side effects) and be wary of universal five-star ratings without nuance.

## Counterfeit products and marketplace risks

Counterfeits often mimic legitimate brands closely but may contain inert fillers or harmful contaminants. Purchasing from verified suppliers and checking batch codes or third-party lab results reduces risk. If a product seems much cheaper than typical retail or lacks traceability, avoid using it and preserve packaging for reporting.

## Dangerous diet pills

Weight-loss products are among the highest-risk categories. Regulators have repeatedly found undeclared pharmaceutical stimulants and banned compounds in some diet pills. Red flags include dramatic before-and-after photos with no verified timelines, promises of effortless weight loss, or claims of FDA approval for dietary supplements.

## Practical verification steps

- Check for third-party testing reports, GMP certification, or listing on recognized regulatory registers.
- Verify study citations and ensure the research supports the exact ingredient, dose, and form used by the product.
- Consult qualified healthcare professionals when combining supplements with medications or managing chronic conditions.
