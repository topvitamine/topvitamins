# Seasonal Supplements: What to Take in Summer vs Winter

As seasons change, so do physiological demands. Adjusting supplements seasonally helps bridge gaps that arise from differences in sunlight exposure, activity levels, and dietary patterns. ## Understanding seasonal supplements

Seasonal supplements are chosen to address common environmental stresses and lifestyle shifts. In summer, increased UV exposure and heat stress can raise antioxidant needs and affect skin health. In winter, reduced sunlight often lowers vitamin D synthesis and can increase susceptibility to respiratory infections and low mood. Tailoring supplementation to these patterns can support immunity, energy, and recovery.

## Summer vitamins: what to focus on

Key nutrients in warmer months include vitamin D, vitamin C, and antioxidants such as vitamin E, beta-carotene, and polyphenols. Although vitamin D is produced in the skin after sun exposure, factors such as sunscreen use, skin pigmentation, and latitude can limit synthesis, so monitoring levels is useful. Vitamin C contributes to collagen formation and antioxidant protection for skin exposed to ultraviolet radiation. Antioxidants help neutralize reactive oxygen species generated by UV and heat stress, supporting cellular repair.

Practical evidence-based considerations:

- Vitamin D: test if possible; supplementation may be warranted for insufficient levels.
- Vitamin C and antioxidants: useful adjuncts for skin health and protection against oxidative damage.

## Winter vitamins: essential nutrients

In colder months prioritize vitamin D, zinc, and B-complex vitamins. Lower sunlight exposure decreases endogenous vitamin D production, which is associated with immune and musculoskeletal function. Zinc plays a well-established role in supporting innate and adaptive immune responses. B vitamins (particularly B12 and folate) participate in energy metabolism and neurotransmitter synthesis, which can influence fatigue and cognitive function when intake or status is low.

Evidence-based tips:

- Vitamin D supplementation is commonly recommended where deficiency is prevalent; dosing should consider baseline levels.
- Zinc supplementation can reduce duration of acute respiratory symptoms when started early, according to clinical trials.

## Seasonal immune boosters

A year-round approach to immune resilience includes maintaining adequate micronutrient status and supporting the gut microbiome. Probiotics have been associated with modest reductions in respiratory tract infections in some studies. Regular intake of vitamin C and zinc at recommended levels supports immune competence. Balance and avoidance of excessive dosing are important to minimize adverse effects.
