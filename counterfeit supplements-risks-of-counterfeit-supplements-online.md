## Introduction

However, this convenience has been accompanied by an increase in counterfeit supplements—unauthorized products that imitate legitimate brands but may contain ineffective, contaminated, or harmful ingredients. ## Health and safety risks

Counterfeit supplements can present multiple hazards. They may contain undeclared pharmaceutical agents, heavy metals, microbial contaminants, or incorrect dosages of active ingredients. Consequences reported in regulatory casework include allergic reactions, acute toxicity, organ injury (notably liver and kidney), and treatment failures when a deficient nutrient is not actually present. The public-health impact extends to undermining confidence in legitimate products and complicating clinical care when patients present with unexpected symptoms or drug interactions.

## Common red flags for counterfeit products

When assessing an online listing or a received product, the following indicators are commonly associated with low-quality or counterfeit supplements:

- Overpromising claims (e.g., guaranteed cures or extreme results) that are inconsistent with established evidence.
- Prices that are substantially lower than typical market rates without clear reason.
- Labels missing batch numbers, expiry dates, full ingredient lists, manufacturer contact information, or bearing typographical errors.
- Seller anonymity or lack of transparent return and shipping policies.

Regulatory agencies occasionally publish enforcement actions; these can provide examples of typical fraudulent patterns and ingredients that have been found in seized products.

## How to verify authenticity before purchase

A structured check can reduce risk:

1. Confirm manufacturer credentials and Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) claims on an official company site.
2. Look for third-party testing seals (e.g., USP, NSF, ConsumerLab) and verify the seal on the certifier’s website.
3. Use QR codes or batch lookup features when available to trace production data.
4. Prefer sellers with clear business information and verifiable reviews from independent platforms.

## Physical checks after delivery

Inspect packaging quality, compare the product appearance with official images, and note smell, color, and tablet/capsule integrity. Simple at-home checks (e.g., dissolution in warm water) can sometimes reveal nonstandard formulations, though laboratory testing is definitive.

## Practical precautions

Use secure payment methods, retain purchase documentation and batch numbers, and consult a healthcare professional before starting new supplements—especially if you have chronic conditions or take medications. When reporting suspected counterfeit products, provide batch information and receipts to the relevant health authority.
