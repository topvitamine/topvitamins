# Zinc Benefits: Why This Mineral Is Essential for Your Health Zinc is a trace mineral required by every cell in the body and is involved in many physiological processes. Although needed in relatively small amounts, zinc benefits span immune function, tissue repair, growth, and enzymatic reactions. Because the body has limited zinc storage, regular dietary intake or supplementation may be necessary to maintain adequate levels. ## What is zinc and how it works Zinc acts as a cofactor for over 300 enzymes that influence metabolism, DNA synthesis, and cell division. It supports immune responses by helping to activate T-cells and other defense mechanisms. Zinc also contributes to skin integrity and wound healing through effects on collagen synthesis and inflammation modulation. In populations with limited dietary diversity, low zinc bioavailability (for example, from high-phytate diets) can increase deficiency risk. Clinical assessment and dietary review are important for diagnosis. ## Dietary sources and absorption Good food sources of zinc include oysters, red meat, poultry, pumpkin seeds, lentils, and chickpeas. Animal-based sources generally provide zinc with higher bioavailability than plant sources. Combining plant-based zinc sources with strategies to reduce phytate content (soaking, fermentation) can improve absorption. ## Supplements: forms and considerations Zinc supplements are available in several forms (zinc gluconate, zinc picolinate, zinc citrate) with similar overall efficacy for addressing mild deficiency.
