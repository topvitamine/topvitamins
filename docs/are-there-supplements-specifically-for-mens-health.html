## Introduction Men’s health involves specific nutritional and lifestyle considerations to maintain physical, mental, and hormonal balance. Dietary supplements can help address gaps in intake and support areas such as muscle maintenance, bone health, immune function, and cognitive performance. This concise, evidence-based overview examines vitamins, minerals, herbal options, and performance-focused supplements that are commonly recommended for men. ## Understanding men’s unique needs Men typically have higher lean muscle mass and different hormonal profiles than women, which affects nutrient demands. Adequate protein and certain micronutrients—especially vitamins B12 and D, zinc, and magnesium—play key roles in energy metabolism, testosterone synthesis, and muscle function. For practical guidance on antioxidant strategies and vitamin C delivery methods, see What is Liposomal Vitamin C?. ## Key vitamins and minerals - Vitamin D: Supports bone and muscle health and has links to reduced cardiometabolic risk when levels are adequate. Men in low-sunlight regions often benefit from measured supplementation. - Vitamin K: Works with vitamin D to regulate calcium deposition and support vascular and skeletal health. - Zinc and magnesium: Important for enzymatic reactions, immune function, and aspects of reproductive health; deficiencies can affect energy and hormone balance. - Vitamin C and other antioxidants: Help mitigate oxidative stress that contributes to aging and chronic disease risk. ## Herbal and specialty supplements Herbals such as ginseng and ashwagandha have been studied for effects on fatigue, stress resilience, and measures of well-being. Saw palmetto is commonly investigated for urinary and prostate-related symptoms in older men. Adaptogens like ashwagandha may influence stress hormones and subjective energy, though individual responses vary and evidence quality differs across studies. ## Omega-3s and cognitive/cardiovascular health Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA and DHA) are associated with lower triglycerides, anti-inflammatory effects, and support for cognitive function. Regular intake—via diet or standardized supplements—can be part of a cardiovascular- and brain-health strategy for men, especially when dietary fish intake is limited. For related regimen ideas, consider seasonal planning such as in New Year supplement planning. ## Supplements for physical performance Creatine, BCAAs, beta-alanine, and L-citrulline have evidence supporting improved strength, reduced fatigue, or enhanced recovery in resistance and high-intensity training contexts. Creatine is among the most consistently supported supplements for augmenting lean mass and strength when combined with resistance training. ## Practical considerations Choosing supplements should be guided by assessed needs, dietary intake, and lab testing where relevant.
